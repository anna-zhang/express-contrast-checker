Graphical Objects

Regular text is defined as 17 points or smaller for regular font weight and 13 points or smaller for bold font weight.

Large text is considered 18 points or larger for regular font weight and 14 points or larger for bold font weight.

Graphical objects include essential icons and other visuals crucial for understanding conveyed information.

For WCAG AA (minimum level) compliance, there should be a contrast ratio of at least 4.5:1 for regular text, 3:1 for large text, and 3:1 for graphical objects.

Achieving WCAG AAA (enhanced level) standards involves a contrast ratio of at least 7:1 is required for regular text, while large text should maintain a minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1. There are no specific AAA standards for graphical objects, so if the 3:1 contrast ratio for AA standards is met, the results indicate a passing status.

Contrast is vital in design to make information clear for everyone, including those with visual impairments; it refers to the perceived brightness difference between foreground and background colors. A contrast checker ensures that text and graphics meet WCAG accessibility standards, maintaining legibility, and enhancing inclusivity for diverse audiences.
